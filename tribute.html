Fritz Haber

The father of modern fertilizer and chemical warfares

Fritz Haber was a german chemist popularly known for the invention of the Haber-Bosh process(the method used to synthesize ammonia from nitrogen and hydrogen gas,) that brought about the invention of modern fertilizer and chemical warfare. He received a nobel prize in chemistry in 1918 for his great invention and was considered the father of chemical warfarefor his years of pioneering work and developing and weaponizing chlorine and other poisonous gases during world war 1.

Below is a short timeline of of events in Fritz Habers life:

  • 1868-Born December 9.
  • 1886- Began University in Germany.
  • 1891-Received his doctorate in chemistry
  • 1898-Wrote his first book, Grundriss der technischen elektrochemie auf theoretischer grundlage.
  • 1894-1911- Haber and his assistant Robert Le Rossignol invented the Haber-Bosch process.
  • 1914-WW1 and the "manifesto of the Ninety- three" was signed by Haber and 92 other inventors of the time.
  • 1915- Haber was present at the second battle of Ypres in belgium. The first release of chlorine gas in the battle field.
  • 1919-1923-Haber continued to work in chemical developments for Germany and other countries alike.
  • 1923-Haber leaves Dahlem due to concerns of the rising socialist party.
  • 1934-January 29 at age 65, Haber dies of heart failure.
